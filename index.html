Our Story

A family inn, kept for grown-ups.

The Ashbrooke has been stewarded by the Anderson family since 2001. A quarter century later, it remains the one Door County hotel built entirely around couples and adult travelers, with no children in the building and no compromise on quiet.

The Anderson Family Egg Harbor · Since 2001

Fred and Debi Anderson took on The Ashbrooke in 2001 and have run it as a true family hotel ever since. Day to day, the inn is shaped by the people who own it, not by a corporate playbook.

That family thread runs deep in Door County. The Andersons' daughter, Kirstin Seaquist, married into the Seaquist Orchards family, one of the peninsula's best-known names in cherry growing. It is the kind of local root that you feel the moment you arrive: this is a hotel run by neighbors, for travelers who want the real Door County.

Every choice here follows from one decision made twenty-five years ago. The Ashbrooke would be a place for two. Anniversaries, milestone birthdays, honeymoons, elopements, and simple escapes from a full house at home.

Reserved for Two

Door County has more than a hundred places to stay. One of them keeps every room for couples.

Resorts here fill with families. Inns fill with weekend crowds. The Ashbrooke chose a quieter path. Every one of the thirty-six rooms is set aside for two adults, so the whole property holds the calm you came north to find.

Come for the anniversary you have been planning all year. Come for the morning with nowhere to be. Come for the version of Door County you cannot get anywhere else.

The Door County Experience

Egg Harbor, just outside your door.

Egg Harbor sits on the bay side of the Door County peninsula, a village of orchards, harbors, and water views that draws travelers back season after season. The Ashbrooke puts you in the middle of it, then gives you a quiet room to return to.

Spend the morning among the cherry orchards the peninsula is famous for, including the Seaquist family farms our own family married into. Drive the shoreline to Sister Bay and Gills Rock. Watch the sunset over the water with nothing on the schedule but dinner.

  • Orchards & farm markets a short drive into the heart of Wisconsin cherry country
  • Harbor & waterfront minutes from the village marina and the bay
  • Galleries, dining & shops walkable from the inn on Egg Harbor Road
  • Quiet evenings home a fireplace and a whirlpool tub waiting at the end of the day

Good to Know

Questions before you book.

Is The Ashbrooke really adults-only?
Yes. The Ashbrooke is reserved for adults and couples across all of its rooms, with no children accommodated in the building. It is the one Door County hotel built entirely around grown-up getaways, which is exactly what keeps the property quiet and unhurried.
What time is check-in and check-out?
We will confirm exact check-in and check-out times with your reservation. If you are arriving early or need a later departure, let us know when you book and we will do our best to make it work around the day's schedule.
Which rooms have whirlpool tubs and fireplaces?
Many of our rooms include an in-room whirlpool tub, and several suites and studios add a gas fireplace. Each of the six room styles is described above. If a whirlpool tub or fireplace is a must for your stay, mention it when you book and we will match you to the right room.
How many rooms does the inn have?
The Ashbrooke has thirty-six rooms across six distinct styles, from the Whirlpool Fireplace Suite to the ground-floor Garden Room. Every one is set aside for two adults.
What is there to do nearby?
You are in the village of Egg Harbor, walking distance to galleries, dining, and shops, and a short drive from the harbors, orchards, and waterfront that Door County is known for. Sister Bay, Fish Creek, and the Seaquist cherry orchards are all within easy reach.
Why book directly with the hotel?
Booking direct connects you with the Anderson family who own and run The Ashbrooke. You reach the people who know the rooms best, and there is no third-party layer between you and your reservation. Call (920) 868-3113 or email reservations@ashbrooke.net.
